The room grew quiet as the final breath left her lips. She did not struggle. She simply let go, as if the weight of the years had become too heavy to carry another moment.

He held her hand until the warmth faded. There was nothing left to say. The clock on the wall kept time for a world that no longer included her.

Outside, rain traced slow paths down the window. Inside, grief settled into every corner like dust that would never be swept away.
