Chapter Seven — Voices in the Rain

The old stone bridge was closed for repair, so Alice and Bob took the long way by the lake, arguing about whether a machine could ever sound like it meant what it said.

## Scene 1

The rain against the café window made a soft percussion as Alice closed her laptop. "We cannot ship this without a voice that sounds human," she said, rubbing her eyes. Bob whispered, "Then we stop treating speech like a checkbox." The narrator would later call this the moment Resonara stopped being a side project.

Outside, streetlights smeared gold across wet asphalt. Alice stood, pacing between the counter and the empty tables. "Said Alice," Bob joked, mocking her habit of narrating her own life. She shouted — actually shouted — "I am not narrating!" Then quieter: "I am terrified we will ship something that sounds like twenty ten."

Bob opened a notebook filled with speaker tags and pause timings. "Narrator stays warm. Alice is bright. I can be rougher." He tapped the page. "If the seams click, listeners leave. If the prosody dies at every chunk, we failed." Alice nodded. The espresso machine hissed like a small dragon clearing its throat.

They argued about dictionaries: PostgreSQL, Kubernetes, nginx, names from customers in Zürich and São Paulo. "People hear care in pronunciation," Alice said. Bob whispered, "Then we care out loud." She almost smiled. Almost.

By midnight the demo chapter ran without a single audible seam. Alice played it twice. "Again," she said. Bob whispered the same line as the recording, and for a second the café held two voices — one human, one nearly so — and neither flinched at the boundaries.
